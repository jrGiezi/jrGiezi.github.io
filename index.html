About

I graduated university with a degree in Music Industry Management, however, upon leaving school I quickly came to realize I was more interested in photography, writing, and the outdoors than I was in managing a band or artist.

While floundering about for a new career path, I fell in love with coffee shops. I figured this might be the way to go, seeing as how I'd wanted to own and operate my own third-wave coffee establishment back home in downtown Detroit. Alas, barista life does not pay the bills (unless you truly wish to have multiple roommates well into your 30s).

During this time, I came to realize several things. I realized that, while I had an eye for photography, I desired more stability in knowing where my monthly incoming was...well, coming from. Remaining a barista would be a hindrance to actually opening my own shop in the future as well, given the need for a financial kick start.

After much deliberation, I came to two paths: carpentry or coding. And while I love the instant gratification of physically making something from a material like wood, the path of coding, though daunting, was too enticing and intriguing to pass up. So here I am, trying each day to figure out what it's all about.
